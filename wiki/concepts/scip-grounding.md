# SCIP grounding

**The survey axis.** A code-comprehension tool is only as trustworthy as its symbol layer. One way to
get a compiler-grade one is a [SCIP](https://github.com/sourcegraph/scip) index — a precise record of
every definition, reference, and moniker. This axis turned out to be an **asymmetry, not a parallel**:
of the two tools in this wiki, only wikify-repo actually grounds on SCIP.

## Who actually uses SCIP
- **wikify-repo — SCIP *is* the grounding substrate.** Its pipeline runs a real SCIP indexer
  (scip-python / scip-clang) as a first-class stage and treats the resulting symbol table as ground
  truth: every citation in the generated wiki must resolve to a real SCIP symbol (the citation-lint
  gate), and cross-file references are followed through SCIP **monikers**. SCIP is not optional — it
  is what makes that wiki grounded rather than a plausible summary. (Its implementations are linked
  below.)
- **graphify — does *not* ground on SCIP.** graphify grounds on deterministic AST extractors, not
  SCIP. It ships a module named [`scip_ingest`](../code/graphify/concepts/graphify-scip_ingest.md),
  but at commit `983da3c` that module is a **dormant, un-wired skeleton**: its own docstring says it is
  a *"simplified subset"*, *"NOT a full SCIP protobuf implementation"*, and *"Not wired to the CLI in
  this phase"*; it parses a simplified, **LLM-generated** SCIP-*style* JSON (not real indexer output),
  is called by no production path (only `tests/test_scip_ingest.py`), and is named in none of
  graphify's docs. It is therefore **deliberately not listed as an implementation below** — it is an
  aspirational lane, not real SCIP grounding.

> [!important]
> The real finding on this axis is the contrast itself: **grounding on a compiler-grade index is a
> deliberate architectural choice, and only wikify-repo makes it.** graphify reaches broad
> multi-source, multi-language coverage precisely by *not* depending on SCIP — trading compiler-grade
> precision for AST-based breadth (see [multi-language-extraction](multi-language-extraction.md)).
