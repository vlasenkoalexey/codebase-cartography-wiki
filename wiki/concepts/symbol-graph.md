# Symbol/call graph

**The survey axis.** Every tool here turns a codebase into a graph whose nodes are symbols
(functions, classes, files) and whose edges are relationships (calls, contains, inherits,
references). The graph is where "comprehension" becomes computable — centrality, neighborhoods,
paths. This page compares *what the graph is for* in each tool.

## How the implementations differ
- **wikify-repo — the graph is an *intermediate* that drives synthesis.** Its `graph` stage builds a
  symbol graph (call/reference edges, plus class-hierarchy "virtual" edges recovered by hierarchy
  analysis) and uses **centrality** over that graph to decide which subsystems deserve a deep
  concept page and to assemble each page's citation subgraph (a "packet"). The graph is machinery;
  the product is the wiki.
- **graphify — the graph *is* the product.** `build` assembles nodes and edges into a persisted
  NetworkX knowledge graph (with careful node-identity reconciliation), and `symbol_resolution`
  conservatively recovers cross-file call edges (import-guided EXTRACTED edges, bare-name INFERRED
  edges, with a god-node guard against over-linking). That graph is then queried, clustered, and
  served directly.

> [!inferred]
> Both build the same *kind* of structure, but the direction of value is opposite: wikify-repo
> consumes its graph internally to write prose, whereas graphify persists and exposes its graph as
> the artifact users query. graphify therefore invests more in identity reconciliation and
> conservative edge recovery (false edges would surface to users), while wikify-repo invests more in
> centrality/packeting (the graph feeds an LLM synthesis step).
